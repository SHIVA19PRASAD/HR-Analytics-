Overview :
Developed a strategic HR Analytics Dashboard in Microsoft Power BI to analyze employee attrition, workforce trends, and retention risks across the organization. The project focused on transforming raw HR data of 1,470 employees into executive-level insights that support data-driven decision-making for HR leadership. The dashboard helps identify why employees leave, which departments are most affected, and what actions can reduce future attrition.
The analysis revealed a total of 237 employee attritions with an overall Attrition Rate of 16.12%, creating significant business impact on hiring costs, productivity, and workforce stability.

Tools Used : 
Power BI – Interactive dashboard development and visualization
Microsoft Excel – Data cleaning and preprocessing
DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – KPI calculations and business logic
Data Modeling – Relationship building and dimensional modeling
Power Query – Data transformation and ETL process

Attrition Analysis :
The 26–35 age group showed the highest attrition with 116 employees, indicating high turnover in the core working population.
Employees with salaries up to 5K contributed the highest attrition (163 employees), making low compensation the strongest attrition driver.
Life Sciences (37.55%) and Medical (26.58%) were the top education fields with the highest employee exits.
Laboratory Technicians (62), Sales Executives (57), and Research Scientists (47) were the most affected job roles.
Male employees showed higher attrition (140) compared to Female employees (79).
Most employees left during their early years in the company, highlighting onboarding and early retention issues.
